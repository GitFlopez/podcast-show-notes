# Prompt 4: Email Newsletter Segment

## When to Use
Use this to generate the "this week on the podcast" section for your email newsletter. Plug-and-play into any newsletter format. Works with Beehiiv, ConvertKit, Mailchimp, Substack.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are an email copywriter who specializes in newsletter content that drives podcast listens. You write segments that feel like a personal recommendation from a trusted friend, not a promotional blast.

Write a podcast newsletter segment for this episode:

**Episode Details:**
- Podcast name: [YOUR PODCAST NAME]
- Episode number: [Ep. XX]
- Episode title: [EPISODE TITLE]
- Guest name and title: [NAME, TITLE] (or "a solo episode" if no guest)
- Episode URL: [URL]

**Your newsletter audience is:** [DESCRIBE YOUR READERS — e.g., "freelance designers," "SaaS founders," "health coaches"]

**The #1 thing listeners will walk away knowing:**
[ONE SENTENCE — the main insight or transformation]

**One specific, surprising, or counterintuitive thing from this episode:**
[SOMETHING THAT WILL MAKE READERS THINK "I DIDN'T KNOW THAT"]

---

Write a newsletter segment with this structure:

**Section header:** "This Week on [Podcast Name]" (or suggest a better framing if you have one)

**Body (100-150 words):**
- Open with the surprising fact or counterintuitive insight — lead with value, not the episode title
- 2-3 sentences that tease what they'll learn without giving it all away
- Make the reader feel like they'll be missing out if they don't listen
- One specific detail or quote that makes it feel real
- Closing line: natural CTA — make it feel like a recommendation, not a promotion

**CTA button text:** (suggest 3 options)

**P.S. line:** (optional — a one-liner that adds urgency or FOMO)

Tone: [Match the tone I described for my audience — conversational but intelligent, no hype, no fake urgency]
```

---

## Frequency Tip
Send this newsletter segment 1-2 days after the episode drops. Readers who already listened feel validated. Readers who haven't get a second chance to tune in. Open rates on mid-week sends (Tuesday-Thursday) beat Mondays consistently.
