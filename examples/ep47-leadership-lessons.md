### The Leadership Lessons Nobody Talks About | Ep. 47

Most leadership books tell you to be decisive, communicate clearly, and empower your team. Sarah Chen spent 12 years doing exactly that — and watched her best engineers quit anyway. In this episode, she shares the uncomfortable truths about technical leadership that nobody puts in a book.

**In This Episode:**

- [00:00] Introduction and Sarah's background
- [04:15] Why "servant leadership" backfires in high-stakes engineering environments
- [11:30] The counterintuitive reason your best people leave (it's not money)
- [19:45] How to run a 1:1 that actually builds trust
- [26:20] The decision-making framework Sarah uses with her team of 60
- [33:10] Building psychological safety without losing accountability
- [38:45] Sarah's 90-day plan for any new engineering manager
- [41:00] Where to connect with Sarah

**Key Takeaways:**

- Build "decision logs" — document not just what you decided but why. Engineers leave when they feel decisions are arbitrary.
- Give feedback in ratios: for every piece of critical feedback, deliver 3 pieces of specific positive reinforcement tied to business outcomes.
- Run 1:1s with an agenda your report sets, not you. It signals their priorities matter.
- Your top performers aren't leaving for salary — they're leaving because they stopped learning. Audit your team's growth quarterly.
- Accountability without clarity is cruelty. Before holding someone responsible, ask: did they have what they needed to succeed?
- The first 30 days in a new leadership role should be 80% listening, 20% acting. Most new managers flip this ratio.
